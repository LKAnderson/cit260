Family

Faith

I am very happy to be a member of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. I was fortunate enough to be born into a family that knows and loves the restored gospel. I served a mission for the Church in Chihuahua, Mexico, from 1988 to 1990. 

I have served in various callings over the years, and I am currently serving as the stake technology specialist and assistant stake clerk. I enjoy serving the Lord in whatever way He calls me to serve.

I have a strong testimony of the restored gospel of Jesus Christ. I love the Church and I support its teachings and standards. I look forward to hearing your testimonies and insights this semester, as well as sharing a few things I've come to appreciate over the years. You may wonder how computer programming and spirituality interact with each other. I can tell you, as with probably anything else, they are intertwined in ways you will be amazed to discover.

Professional

I graduated from BYU in 1994 with a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science. I have been a professional software developer for more than 20 years. I started working as a programmer while still at BYU in Provo. After graduating, I stayed on as a full-time programmer at the University for a few more years. Eventually, Christine and I decided it was time to spread our wings a little wider, so we moved to the Phoenix, Arizona area in July 1997, and we have lived here ever since. Over my professional career, I have worked in educational financial operations, telecommunications, financial transaction processing, auto parts, aerospace and avionics, and now, insurance. I currently work at USAA in Phoenix. We do a lot of Java work, and my team's current project is developing a mobile app for iOS and Android. One of the things I love about my profession is getting to know all about the industries I am working in as I develop software tools for people to use to make their lives better.

Computer programming is an extremely creative process. This creativity is one of the things I enjoy most about the work I do. Mastery of the tools of the trade will allow your creativity to flourish. It takes discipline and effort to master these tools, so be patient, and be diligent. You will get there. I promise! 

I am excited to be your instructor this semester! I can't wait to get started.
