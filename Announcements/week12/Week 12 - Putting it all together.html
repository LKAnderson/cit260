Week 12 - Back to Spiritual Creation

Yes, we're going back to Spiritual Creation (also known as software design) this week. Your assignment is to design the data classes you will use for your program. You will then design the user interaction with your program, known as the "functional specification," or "requirements" of your program. This is how your program will work from the user's perspective.

You know everything you need to know to do the class diagram. It should be UML, and you are free to use any of the shortcuts I have demonstrated in my UML this semester (for example, a "{ get set }" after a property in the data section so you don't have to type a getter and setter in the method section). Your design needs to show a base class with at least two child classes. You also need to make it clear which method(s) you're going to use for polymorphism (same method, different implementations).

Functional Specification

The functional spec, or requirements, for your program should describe how the program will interact with the user to carry out the functions of the program. For interactive programs such as this project, it is helpful to follow a simple recipe for your specification:

1. Describe what the computer displays to the user, including any choices the user needs to make.

2. Describe how the user will enter their choice, or input.

3. Describe what the computer will do in response to user's input.

Obviously, the more detailed your spiritual creation is, the easier it will be to write the code. This is because you're putting in the work ahead of time to make decisions about how you want things to work. If you put off making these kinds of decisions, you'll end up having to make these decisions at the same time you're having to make coding decisions, and that tends to drain the brain very quickly!

I have attached a Sample Functional Specification document as an example. This example is from the previous version of the course, where students had to develop a very complex game during the semester. Your projects will not be this complex. However, it provides an example of an appropriate level of detail and organization for your document.

Your program will have fewer features, and therefore fewer interactions with the user to describe. Your UML diagram will be much smaller, too.

Please submit your document in PDF format to ensure it looks like you intended it to look when I view it.
